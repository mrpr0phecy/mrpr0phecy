Anatomy Results & Facts

  • The human body has 206 bones, but babies are born with around 300.
  • Your heart beats about 100,000 times per day, pumping ~7,500 liters of blood.
  • The small intestine is about 6 meters long, while the large intestine is ~1.5 meters.
  • Skin is the largest organ, covering ~2 square meters in adults.
  • The brain uses about 20% of your body’s oxygen and energy.
  • Red blood cells live for ~120 days before being recycled by the spleen.
  • Muscles make up ~40% of body weight; the strongest is the masseter (jaw muscle).
  • The liver performs over 500 vital functions, including detox and metabolism.
  • Every second, your body produces ~25 million new cells.
  • DNA in one human cell, if stretched out, is about 2 meters long.
