Materials Science Results & Facts

  • Steel is an alloy of iron and carbon, balancing strength and ductility.
  • Diamond is the hardest known natural material, rated 10 on the Mohs scale.
  • Graphene is a single layer of carbon atoms, stronger than steel yet flexible.
  • Shape‑memory alloys like Nitinol can return to their original shape when heated.
  • Concrete is the most widely used man‑made material on Earth.
  • Polymers like plastics are long chains of repeating molecules, versatile and lightweight.
  • Composite materials combine properties, e.g. fiberglass (glass + polymer matrix).
  • Glass is an amorphous solid, lacking long‑range crystalline order.
  • Nanomaterials exhibit unique properties due to their tiny size and high surface area.
  • Superalloys withstand extreme temperatures, used in jet engines and turbines.
