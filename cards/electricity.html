Electricity Results & Facts

  • Lightning bolts can reach temperatures of 30,000 K — hotter than the Sun’s surface.
  • A single bolt of lightning can power a 100‑watt bulb for 3 months.
  • Electric eels can produce shocks of up to 600 volts.
  • Benjamin Franklin’s kite experiment proved lightning is electrical in nature.
  • Direct current (DC) flows one way; alternating current (AC) changes direction many times per second.
  • The longest recorded lightning bolt stretched over 700 km across Brazil.
  • Static electricity is caused by imbalance of charges on surfaces.
  • Electricity travels close to the speed of light in conductors.
  • Hydroelectric dams convert falling water into electrical energy.
  • Solar panels turn photons into electrons via the photovoltaic effect.
