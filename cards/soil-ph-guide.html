Soil & pH Guide

Soil pH and type determine nutrient availability and plant success. Use this guide to match plant groups with their preferred conditions.

pH Categories

  • Strongly Acidic (pH 4.0–5.0) → Blueberries, Cranberries, Azaleas, Rhododendrons, Camellias, Heather, Hydrangeas (for blue flowers)
  • Moderately Acidic (pH 5.0–6.0) → Potatoes, Sweet potatoes, Parsley, Peppers, Strawberries, Raspberries, Hydrangeas (blue/purple)
  • Slightly Acidic to Neutral (pH 6.0–7.0) → Most vegetables (tomatoes, carrots, beans, peas, lettuce, cucumbers, corn), Roses, Lawns, Marigolds, Zinnias
  • Neutral to Slightly Alkaline (pH 7.0–7.5) → Brassicas (cabbage, broccoli, kale, cauliflower, Brussels sprouts), Spinach, Beets, Lilacs, Clematis, Leeks
  • Alkaline (pH 7.5–8.5) → Lavender, Clematis, Asparagus, Artichokes, Mediterranean herbs (oregano, thyme, sage, rosemary), Lilacs

Soil Types

  • Sandy → Carrots, Lettuce, Parsnips, Mediterranean herbs, Radishes, Potatoes, Strawberries
  • Clay → Roses, Cabbages, Daylilies, Willow trees, Hostas, Iris, Beans, Broccoli
  • Loam → Tomatoes, Beans, Peppers, Apples, Most ornamentals, Squash, Corn, Leafy greens
  • Silt → Leafy greens, Shrubs, Grasses, Most vegetables, Willow trees, Moisture-loving plants
  • Chalky → Spinach, Beets, Lilacs, Lavender, Clematis, Cabbage, Broccoli
  • Peaty → Potatoes, Carrots, Brassicas, Salad crops, Rhododendrons, Azaleas, Blueberries
