Period ( . )

Full Stop, Full Point
Primary Uses
  • End declarative sentences
  • End imperative sentences
  • Abbreviations (Mr., Dr., etc.)
  • Decimal points (3.14)
Examples
She went to the store.
Please close the door.
Dr. Smith arrived at 3 p.m.
‚ö†Ô∏è Common Mistakes: Don't use periods after headings, titles (unless part of the title), or acronyms (NASA, FBI).

Comma ( , )

The most versatile punctuation mark
Main Rules
  • Separate items in a list
  • Join independent clauses with conjunctions
  • Set off introductory elements
  • Set off non-essential information
  • Separate coordinate adjectives
Examples
Apples, oranges, and bananas
I wanted to go, but I was tired.
After dinner, we watched a movie.
My brother, who lives in Paris, is visiting.
Oxford Comma
Optional before "and" in lists. Adds clarity.
Common Error
Comma splices: joining clauses without conjunction.
;

Semicolon ( ; )

Stronger than a comma, weaker than a period
Uses
  • Join related independent clauses
  • Separate items in complex lists
  • Use with transitional expressions
Examples
I have a test tomorrow; I need to study.
The team visited Paris, France; Rome, Italy; and Madrid, Spain.
I love coffee; however, I try to limit my intake.
:

Colon ( : )

Introduces or emphasizes what follows
Uses
  • Introduce a list
  • Introduce an explanation
  • Introduce a quotation
  • Separate title and subtitle
  • Time notation (10:30)
Examples
You need three things: patience, practice, and persistence.
Remember this: always be kind.
The meeting starts at 9:00 AM.
'

Apostrophe ( ' )

Shows possession or omission
Rules
  • Singular possessive: cat's toy
  • Plural possessive: cats' toys
  • Contractions: don't (do not)
  • Plurals of letters: Mind your p's and q's
Examples
Sarah's book is on the table.
The students' projects were excellent.
It's (it is) time to go.
‚ùå Common Errors: Don't use apostrophes for plural nouns (apples, not apple's), possessive pronouns (its, yours, theirs), or decades (1990s, not 1990's unless possessive).

Quotation Marks ( " " )

Double quotes for direct speech and titles
Uses
  • Direct quotations
  • Titles of short works
  • Words used in special ways
  • Scare quotes (for irony)
Single Quotes ( ' ' )
Use within double quotes for quotations within quotations.
Examples
She said, "I'll be there soon."
Read "The Raven" by Edgar Allan Poe.
He called it a "revolutionary" idea.
She asked, "Did he say 'I love you'?"
Punctuation Placement: Commas and periods go inside quotes in US English.

Parentheses ( )

Enclose supplemental information
Uses
  • Additional explanations
  • Acronym definitions
  • Citations (APA, MLA)
  • Numbered lists in text
  • Mathematical expressions
Examples
The conference (which was held online) was successful.
NASA (National Aeronautics and Space Administration)
Several studies support this claim (Smith, 2020; Jones, 2021).
