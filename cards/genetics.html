Genetics Results & Facts

  • If stretched out, DNA in one human cell is about 2 meters long.
  • The human genome contains about 3 billion base pairs.
  • Only ~2% of human DNA codes for proteins; the rest is regulatory or non‑coding.
  • Humans share ~98.8% of their DNA with chimpanzees.
  • Mitochondrial DNA is inherited almost exclusively from the mother.
  • There are ~20,000–25,000 protein‑coding genes in the human genome.
  • DNA replication makes ~50 nucleotides per second in human cells.
  • Some bacteria can swap genes horizontally, unlike humans who inherit vertically.
  • Telomeres protect chromosome ends but shorten with age.
  • CRISPR technology allows precise editing of genetic sequences.
