Thermodynamics Results & Facts

  • The Second Law of Thermodynamics states entropy always increases in isolated systems.
  • Absolute zero (0 K) is the point where molecular motion theoretically stops.
  • Engines can never be 100% efficient due to unavoidable energy losses.
  • The Carnot cycle defines the maximum efficiency possible for a heat engine.
  • Heat flows spontaneously from hot to cold, never the reverse.
  • The Sun’s energy output is ~3.8×10²⁶ watts, driving Earth’s climate.
  • Entropy is often described as a measure of disorder or randomness.
  • Refrigerators work by transferring heat from inside to outside using work input.
  • Steam engines powered the Industrial Revolution using thermodynamic principles.
  • Thermodynamics applies to black holes: they have entropy and temperature.
