Microbiology Results & Facts

  • There are more bacterial cells in your gut than stars in the Milky Way.
  • Microbes make up ~60% of Earth’s biomass.
  • Some bacteria can survive extreme radiation and temperatures (extremophiles).
  • Viruses are not technically alive — they need a host to replicate.
  • Yeast helped humans brew beer and bake bread thousands of years ago.
  • Antibiotic resistance is one of the biggest challenges in modern medicine.
  • Microbes recycle nutrients, making ecosystems sustainable.
  • Some bacteria glow in the dark (bioluminescence).
  • Archaea are distinct from bacteria and thrive in extreme environments.
  • Microbiomes influence mood, immunity, and even behavior.
