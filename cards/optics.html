Optics Results & Facts

  • Light travels at ~299,792 km/s in a vacuum.
  • Rainbows are formed by refraction, reflection, and dispersion of light in water droplets.
  • Fiber optics use total internal reflection to transmit data at high speeds.
  • The human eye lens changes shape to focus at different distances.
  • Mirages occur due to refraction of light in layers of hot air.
  • Polarized sunglasses reduce glare by blocking certain light orientations.
  • Lasers produce coherent light waves all in phase.
  • Telescopes use lenses or mirrors to magnify distant objects.
  • Diffraction causes light to bend around obstacles and create interference patterns.
  • Optical illusions exploit how the brain interprets visual signals.
