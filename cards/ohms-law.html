Ohm's Law Facts

The Formula

Ohm's Law is expressed as: V = I × R

V = I × R

Where:
V = Voltage (Volts)
I = Current (Amperes)
R = Resistance (Ohms)

Power Formula

Electrical power is calculated as: P = V × I

P = V × I

Combined with Ohm's Law, power can also be expressed as:
P = I² × R
P = V² / R

History

Ohm's Law was formulated by German physicist Georg Simon Ohm in 1827. He discovered the relationship between voltage, current, and resistance through a series of experiments with electrical circuits.

Practical Applications

Ohm's Law is fundamental to electrical engineering and is used in designing circuits, calculating wire sizes, selecting components, troubleshooting electrical problems, and ensuring electrical safety.
